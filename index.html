Agreements are consent-driven collaborative documents that detail
how a team agrees to work together. Teams that make agreements,
keep, and share agreements are more effective.

Go from a blank page to something
everybody loves in five simple steps.

Draft

Anyone on your team can draft and propose an
agreement.

Start from scratch, use a template, or copy a public agreement
and make it your own.

Understand

Everyone has a chance to ask questions.

Before we criticize, let's take a moment to understand what's
on the table and why.

Improve

Now it's time for suggestions.

How can we make the agreement safer, or more
inclusive? The proposer has a chance to refine and revise.

Finalize

Is it safe to try?

This is where we flip the script on the usual top down
approach. Everyone on the team has the right to consent or
object. But we don't get stuck. Objections become resolutions
and teams move forward.

Refresh

Everyone can see what is working and what isn't.

Each agreement has its own feedback rhythm, pulsing the
team on followthrough and satisfaction. When an agreement
expires, the team is prompted to make a choice to refresh,
renew, or retire it.
